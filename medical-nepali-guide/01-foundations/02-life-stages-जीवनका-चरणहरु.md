# Section 2: Life Stages (जीवनका चरणहरु)

See also [Section 9: Women's Health, Pregnancy & Childbirth](../03-special-populations/09-womens-health-pregnancy-childbirth-महिला-स्वास्थ्य-र-प्रसूति.md) for menstruation, pregnancy, and childbirth language; [Section 10: Pediatrics & Child Raising](../03-special-populations/10-pediatrics-child-raising-बाल-स्वास्थ्य-र-हुर्काइ.md) for developmental and pediatric care language; and [Section 21: End of Life, Hospice & Death](../06-context-lifestyle-and-end-of-life/21-end-of-life-hospice-death-अन्तिम-अवस्था-र-मृत्यु.md) for later-life and end-of-life terminology.

## 1. Life Stages

- Fetus / Unborn Baby: *Bhroon* (भ्रूण - formal/clinical) or *Garbha ko bacha* (गर्भको बच्चा).

- Newborn (0-28 days): *Nawal jaat sishu* (नवजात शिशु)

- Infant / Baby: *Bacha* (बच्चा) or *Babunani* (बाबुनानी - affectionate catch-all for a little child)

- Small boy (affectionate): *Babu* / *Babhu* (बाबु)

- Small girl (affectionate): *Nani* (नानी)

- Toddler/Small Child: *Sano bacha* (सानो बच्चा)

- Adolescent/Teenager: *Kishor* (किशोर - boy) / *Kishori* (किशोरी - girl) or *Tinejar* (टिनेजर - loanword).

- Young Adult / Youth: *Jawan* (जवान)

- Adult: *Bayaska* (वयस्क)

- Middle-Aged: *Adhaibainse* (अधबैंसे)

- Old Age: *Briddha* (वृद्ध - formal), *Jestha nagarik* (ज्येष्ठ नागरिक - senior citizen), or *Budho* (बुढो - old man) / *Budhi* (बुढी - old woman).

## 2. Family and Relationship Terms

- Father: *Buwa* (बुवा) or *Baa* (बा)

- Mother: *Aama* (आमा)

- Son: *Chhora* (छोरा)

- Daughter: *Chhori* (छोरी)

- Older brother: *Dai* (दाइ)

- Younger brother: *Bhai* (भाइ)

- Older sister: *Didi* (दिदी)

- Younger sister: *Bahini* (बहिनी)

- Husband: *Sriman* (श्रीमान् - formal) or *Logne* (लोग्ने - colloquial)

- Wife: *Srimati* (श्रीमती - formal) or *Swasni* (स्वास्नी - colloquial, context-sensitive)

- Friend: *Saathi* (साथी)

- Relative / extended family member: *Aafanta* (आफन्त) or *Naatedar* (नातेदार)

- Grandfather: *Baje* (बाजे) or *Hajurbuwa* (हजुरबुवा - very respectful)

- Grandmother: *Bajai* (बज्यै) or *Hajurama* (हजुरआमा - very respectful)

- Grandson: *Naati* (नाति)

- Granddaughter: *Naatini* (नातिनी)

- Teacher: *Shikshak* (शिक्षक), *Shikshika* (शिक्षिका), or *Teacher* (टिचर)

- Student: *Vidyarthi* (विद्यार्थी)

- Boss / superior: *Boss* (बोस), *Saheb* (साहेब), or *Hakim* (हाकिम - older-style / official superior)

- Employee / staff member: *Karmachari* (कर्मचारी)

- Patient attendant / caregiver who came along: *Kuruwa* (कुरुवा) or *Herne manche* (हेर्ने मान्छे)

- Unknown person / stranger: *Nachineko manche* (नचिनेको मान्छे)

## 3. Common Forms of Address in Clinical Settings

Kinship words are often used for non-relatives in Nepal as respectful or warm forms of address. In a clinical setting, it is usually safest to begin more formally and then follow the family’s tone.

- All-purpose respectful address: *Hajur* (हजुर) or *Ji* (जी)

- Small boy: *Babu* / *Babhu* (बाबु)

- Small girl: *Nani* (नानी)

- Younger male / younger man: *Bhai* (भाइ)

- Younger female / younger woman: *Bahini* (बहिनी)

- Slightly older or peer-aged man: *Dai* (दाइ)

- Slightly older or peer-aged woman: *Didi* (दिदी)

- Adult man in formal or urban contexts: *Sir* / *Sar* (सर)

- Adult woman in formal or urban contexts: *Ma'am* / *Mam* (म्याम) or *Madam* (म्याडम)

- Middle-aged or older woman: *Aama* (आमा)

- Middle-aged or older man: *Buwa* (बुवा) or *Baa* (बा)

- Very elderly woman: *Hajurama* (हजुरआमा)

- Very elderly man: *Hajurbuwa* (हजुरबुवा)

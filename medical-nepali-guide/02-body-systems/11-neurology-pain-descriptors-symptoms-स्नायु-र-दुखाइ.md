# Section 11: Neurology, Pain Descriptors & Symptoms (स्नायु र दुखाइ)

## 1. Neurological Symptoms

- Fainting / Unconscious: *Behos hune* (बेहोस हुने) or *Murccha parne* (मूर्छा पर्ने - to swoon/faint).

- Dizziness / Lightheaded: *Ringata laagne* (रिङ्गटा लाग्ने) or *Chakkar laagera ladeko* (dizzy and fell).

- Vertigo (Room spinning): *Chakkar aaune* (चक्कर आउने) or *Ghumeko jasto lagne*.

- Headache (General): *Taauko dukheko* (टाउको दुखेको).

- Migraine / Severe half-head ache: *Giruwa dukhne* (गिरुवा दुख्ने) or *Aadha taauko dukhne*.

- Stroke (The medical event): *Mastiskaghat* (मस्तिष्कघात - formal/clinical brain stroke).

- Stroke / Having had a stroke (The condition): *Pakshyaghat* (पक्षघात - formal paralysis), or highly colloquially using the loanword *Paralysis bhayo* (प्यारालाइसिस भयो).

- Stroke symptoms: *Mukh baangiyeko* (मुख बाङ्गिएको - face drooping), *Boli ladbadaayeko* (बोली लडबडाएको - slurred speech), *Haat-khutta lulo bhayeko* (हात-खुट्टा लुलो भएको - limbs going weak/floppy).

## 2. Specific Pain Descriptors

- Nepali has highly specific onomatopoeic words for different types of pain.

- Throbbing / Pulsating pain: *Tonk-tonk garne* (टन्क-टन्क गर्ने) or *Tankanay*.

- Stabbing / Pricking pain: *Chasakka ghochne* (चसक्क घोच्ने).

- Burning pain: *Polne* (पोल्ने) or *Polcha* (पोल्छ).

- Aching / Deep bone pain: *Kat-kat khane* (कट-कट खाने - usually associated with joint/bone aches).

- Tingling / Pins and needles: *Jham-jham garne* (झम-झम गर्ने) or *Jhamjhamaune*.

- Cramping / Twisted pain: *Baadiyeko* (बाडिएको).

# Section 19: Hospital Hierarchy, Admin & Places (अस्पताल र बिमा)

## 1. Places in the Hospital

- Emergency Room (ER): *Apatkalin kaksha* (आपतकालीन कक्ष) or *Emergency* (इमर्जेन्सी).

- Outpatient Department (OPD): *Bahiranga bibhag* (बहिरंग विभाग) or *OPD* (ओ.पी.डी.).

- Inpatient Department (IPD) / Medical Ward: *Antaranga bibhag* (अन्तरंग विभाग) or *Ward* (वार्ड).

- Laboratory: *Prayogshala* (प्रयोगशाला) or *Lab* (ल्याब).

- Pharmacy: *Aushadhi pasal* (औषधि पसल) or *Pharmacy* (फार्मेसी).

- Operating Room / Theater: *Salya-kriya kaksha* (शल्यक्रिया कक्ष) or *OT* (ओ.टी.).

- Delivery Room: *Sutkeri kaksha* (सुत्केरी कक्ष) or *Prasuti griha* (प्रसूति गृह).

- Postpartum/Maternity Ward: *Sutkeri ward* (सुत्केरी वार्ड) or *Prasuti ward* (प्रसूति वार्ड).

- Waiting Area: *Pratikshya Kaksha* (प्रतीक्षा कक्ष) (formal) or *Kurne thau* (कुर्ने ठाउँ) (common usage).

- Restroom / Toilet: *Sauchalaya* (शौचालय) (formal signage) or *Twailet* (ट्वाइलेट) / *Baath-rum* (बाथरुम) (common usage).

## 2. Health System Facilities

- Local Pharmacy/Clinic: *Medical* (मेडिकल - used to describe local privately-owned pharmacies that often do basic triage).

- Health Post: *Swasthya Chauki* (स्वास्थ्य चौकी - rural government clinic).

- Primary Healthcare Center (PHCC): *Prathamik Swasthya Kendra* (प्राथमिक स्वास्थ्य केन्द्र).

- Hospital: *Aspatal* (अस्पताल).

- District Hospital: *Zilla Aspatal* (जिल्ला अस्पताल).

## 3. Staff Titles

- Doctor: *Chikitsak* (चिकित्सक - formal) or *Daktar* (डाक्टर - common).

- Nurse: *Paricharika* (परिचारिका - highly formal/literary, rarely used in speech) or *Nurse* (नर्स - common).

- Health Assistant (HA): *HA* (एच.ए. - mid-level provider, often runs health posts).

- CMA / ANM: *CMA* (सी.एम.ए.) or *ANM* (ए.एन.एम. - Auxiliary Nurse Midwife - common in rural areas).

- <details>
  <summary>Female Community Health Volunteer: <em>Mahila Swasthya Swayamsewika</em> (महिला स्वास्थ्य स्वयंसेविका) or simply <em>FCHV</em> (एफ.सी.एच.भी.).</summary>
  
  <br>
  
  **FCHV** stands for **Female Community Health Volunteer**. As noted in the vocabulary list, the Nepali term is *Mahila Swasthya Swayamsewika* (महिला स्वास्थ्य स्वयंसेविका). 
  
  In the healthcare system here, FCHVs are an absolutely essential frontline resource. They serve as the primary grassroots connection between local neighborhoods or villages and the formal health sector (like Health Posts and primary care clinics). 
  
  Their role generally focuses on:
  * Maternal and child health education and support.
  * Distributing essential health commodities (like oral rehydration salts, vitamin A, iron pills, and family planning supplies).
  * Promoting immunization campaigns.
  * Acting as the first point of contact to refer patients up the chain to clinics or hospitals to be seen by a physician.
  </details>

- Traditional Birth Attendant: *Sudeni* (सुँडेनी).

## 4. Administration, Instructions & Insurance

- Admit to hospital: *Bharna garne* (भर्ना गर्ने - to admit someone) or *Bharna hune* (भर्ना हुने - to get admitted).

- Discharge: *Discharge garne / Chhutti dine* (छुट्टी दिने - to discharge someone) or *Discharge hune* (to get discharged).

- Referral: *Refer garne* or *Arko aspatal ma pathaune* (अर्को अस्पतालमा पठाउने).

- Discharge/OPD Follow-up instruction: *Pheri aaunu* (फेरि आउनु - come again), *Farkera aaunu* (फर्केर आउनु - come back), or *Aushadhi sidhiyepachi aaunu* (come back when medicine is finished).

- Health Insurance: *Swasthya Bima* (स्वास्थ्य बिमा) or simply *Bima* (बिमा).

- Insurance Card: *Bima card* (बिमा कार्ड).

- Claiming insurance / Discounted: *Paisa chhut hune* (पैसा छुट हुने - getting a discount/coverage) or *Claim garne*.

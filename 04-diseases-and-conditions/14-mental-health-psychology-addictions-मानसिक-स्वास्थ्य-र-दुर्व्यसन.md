## 1. Mental Health Symptoms

- Stress / tension: `तनाव` (*tanaab*) — the most common everyday and clinical word for stress, strain, or psychological pressure.
  Example: `म धेरै तनावमा छु।` (*ma dherai tanaabmaa chhu.*) — "I am under a lot of stress."

- Anxiety / panic: `आत्तिएको` (*aattiyeko*) or `चिन्ता` (*chintaa*) — `आत्तिएको` often describes feeling panicky, overwhelmed, or flustered in the moment; `चिन्ता` is broader worry or anxious concern.
  Example: `म धेरै आत्तिएको र चिन्तित महसुस गर्छु।` (*ma dherai aattiyeko ra chintit mahasus garchhu.*) — "I feel very panicked and worried."

- Depression: `डिप्रेसन` (*dipresan*) or `निराशा` (*niraashaa*) — `डिप्रेसन` is the common loanword for depression; `निराशा` more literally means hopelessness or deep discouragement, so it can overlap with but does not always exactly equal major depression.
  Example: `उहाँ डिप्रेसनमा हुनुहुन्छ जस्तो देखिन्छ।` (*uha dipresanmaa hunuhunchha jasto dekhinchha.*) — "It seems like they are depressed."

- Common depressive idioms: `मन पिर परेको` (*man pir pareko*; lit. "the heart-mind is pained"), `खुसी नलाग्ने` (*khusi nalaagne*), and `एक्लै बस्न मन लाग्ने` (*eklai basna man laagne*) — these are high-yield everyday ways patients describe emotional pain, not enjoying anything, and wanting isolation.
  Example: `मेरो मन पिर परेको छ, खुसी लाग्दैन, र एक्लै बस्न मन लाग्छ।` (*mero man pir pareko chha, khusi laagdaina, ra eklai basna man laagchha.*) — "My heart-mind feels pained, I do not feel happy, and I feel like sitting alone."

- Hallucinations (especially hearing voices): `आवाज सुन्ने` (*aawaaj sunne*) — literally "hearing voices/sounds"; in psychiatric contexts this often refers to auditory hallucinations, but in conversation it still needs clarification.
  Example: `उहाँलाई अरूले नसुन्ने आवाज सुन्ने समस्या छ।` (*uhaalai arule nasunne aawaaj sunne samasya chha.*) — "They have the problem of hearing voices that others do not hear."

- Delusional suspicion / paranoia: `शंका गर्ने` (*shankaa garne*) — literally "to suspect." In clinical use it often points to pathological suspiciousness or paranoia, though in ordinary speech it can also just mean being mistrustful.
  Example: `उहाँ सबैले आफ्नो विरुद्ध कुरा गरिरहेका छन् भनेर शंका गर्नुहुन्छ।` (*uhaa sabai le aafno viruddha kura garirahika chhan bhanera shankaa garnuhunchha.*) — "They suspect that everyone is talking against them."

- Suicidal thoughts: `आत्महत्या गर्ने विचार आएको` (*aatmahatyaa garne bichaar aayeko*) — direct language for suicidal ideation. For the broader distinction between self-harm and suicide language, see the later subsection on self-harm and suicide.
  Example: `मलाई आत्महत्या गर्ने विचार आएको छ।` (*malaai aatmahatyaa garne bichaar aayeko chha.*) — "I have had thoughts of suicide."

## 2. Psychosis, Severe Mental Disturbance, and Colloquial Labels

These words matter because families and communities may use them before anyone uses a formal psychiatric label. Several of them are strongly stigmatizing. In clinician speech, it is usually better to prefer symptom-based descriptions or formal mental-health language when possible.

- Psychosis (formal/clinical): `मनोविकृति` (*manovikriti*) — a technical psychiatric term for psychosis. Most patients and families will not use this word first, but clinicians may use it in explanation or teaching.
  Example: `उहाँमा मनोविकृतिका लक्षण देखिएका छन्।` (*uhaamaa manovikritikaa lakshan dekhiyeka chhan.*) — "They are showing symptoms of psychosis."

- Mental balance disturbed: `मानसिक सन्तुलन बिग्रिएको` (*maanasik santulan bigriyeko*) — a softer, more family-facing way to say someone is mentally unwell or not thinking clearly; less harsh than calling someone "crazy."
  Example: `परिवारले उहाँको मानसिक सन्तुलन बिग्रिएको जस्तो लागेको बतायो।` (*pariwaarle uhaako maanasik santulan bigriyeko jasto laageko bataayo.*) — "The family said it seemed that their mental balance was disturbed."

- Crazy / mad: `पागल` (*paagal*) — extremely common, but strongly stigmatizing. Important to understand because families may say it, but it is usually not good clinician language.
  Example: `गाउँका मानिसहरूले उहाँलाई पागल भनेर बोलाउँछन्।` (*gaaukaa maanisaharule uhaalai paagal bhanera bolaaunchhan.*) — "People in the village call them crazy."

- Madness / craziness: `पागलपन` (*paagalpan*) — the noun form of `पागल`; usually refers to madness, bizarre behavior, or what others see as irrationality.
  Example: `परिवारले यो पागलपन हो कि रोग हो भनेर सोध्छ।` (*pariwaarle yo paagalpan ho ki rog ho bhanera sodhchha.*) — "The family asks whether this is madness or an illness."

- Gone mad / crazed: `बौलाएको` (*baulaayeko*) or `बौलाहा` (*baulaahaa*) — common colloquial labels for someone seen as mentally disturbed, erratic, or out of touch with reality. These are also stigmatizing.
  Example: `छिमेकीहरूले उहाँ बौलाएको भन्न थाले।` (*chhimekiharule uhaa baulaayeko bhanna thaale.*) — "The neighbors started saying that they had gone mad."

- Mind / brain "gone bad": `दिमाग खराब भएको` (*dimaag kharaab bhayeko*) or `दिमाग बिग्रिएको` (*dimaag bigriyeko*) — very common everyday phrasing. It is broad and can refer to severe emotional distress, psychosis, confusion, or behavior others find irrational.
  Example: `उहाँको दिमाग खराब भएको जस्तो छ भनेर परिवार डराएको छ।` (*uhaako dimaag kharaab bhayeko jasto chha bhanera pariwaar daraayeko chha.*) — "The family is frightened, saying it seems like their mind has gone bad."

- Seeing things that are not there: `नभएको कुरा देख्ने` (*nabhayeko kura dekhne*) — a straightforward patient- or family-facing way to describe visual hallucinations.
  Example: `उहाँले नभएको कुरा देख्ने गर्नुभएको छ।` (*uhaale nabhayeko kura dekhne garnubhayeko chha.*) — "They have been seeing things that are not there."

- Talking strangely / nonsensically: `उल्टो-सुल्टो कुरा गर्ने` (*ulto-sulto kura garne*) — a very common everyday phrase for odd, bizarre, or nonsensical speech.
  Example: `राति उहाँ उल्टो-सुल्टो कुरा गर्न थाल्नुहुन्छ।` (*raati uhaa ulto-sulto kura garna thaalnuhunchha.*) — "At night, they start saying strange and nonsensical things."

- Incoherent / disorganized speech: `असम्बद्ध कुरा गर्ने` (*asambaddha kura garne*) — a more formal way to describe speech that lacks connection or coherence.
  Example: `उहाँले असम्बद्ध कुरा गरिरहनुभएको छ।` (*uhaale asambaddha kura garirahanu bhayeko chha.*) — "They are speaking incoherently."

- Feeling followed / watched: `कसैले आफूलाई पछ्याइरहेको जस्तो लाग्ने` (*kasaile aafulai pachhyaairahiko jasto laagne*) — useful for persecutory fears or delusions.
  Example: `उहाँलाई कसैले आफूलाई पछ्याइरहेको जस्तो लाग्छ।` (*uhaalai kasaile aafulai pachhyaairahiko jasto laagchha.*) — "They feel that someone is following them."

- Feeling someone is trying to harm or kill you: `कसैले आफूलाई हानि गर्न खोजिरहेको जस्तो लाग्ने` (*kasaile aafulai haani garna khojirahiko jasto laagne*) or `कसैले आफूलाई मार्न खोजिरहेको जस्तो लाग्ने` (*kasaile aafulai maarna khojirahiko jasto laagne*) — useful persecutory wording when the patient believes others are after them.
  Example: `उहाँलाई कसैले आफूलाई मार्न खोजिरहेको जस्तो लागिरहेको छ।` (*uhaalai kasaile aafulai maarna khojirahiko jasto laagirahiko chha.*) — "They feel that someone is trying to kill them."

- Spirit-possession explanation: `देउता चढेको` (*deuta chadeko*) or `भूतप्रेत लागेको` (*bhut-pret laageko*) — families may use these cultural explanations for severe behavioral change, trance-like states, or psychosis-like symptoms. These expressions are important to recognize even when the clinical explanation is different. See also [Section 22: Traditional Healing & Cultural Beliefs](../06-context-lifestyle-and-end-of-life/22-traditional-healing-cultural-beliefs-परम्परागत-उपचार-र-जनविश्वास.md).
  Example: `परिवारले उहाँलाई भूतप्रेत लागेको हुन सक्छ भन्छ।` (*pariwaarle uhaalai bhut-pret laageko huna sakchha bhanchha.*) — "The family says it may be spirit possession."

## 3. Bipolar Disorder, Mania, and Mood Swings

In everyday Nepali, there is no single universally used colloquial word that cleanly separates bipolar disorder from psychosis. Families often describe the pattern instead: periods of unusually high energy, little sleep, nonstop talking, impulsive behavior, irritability, and later depression. Because of that, symptom-based descriptions are often clearer than labels alone.

- Bipolar disorder: `बाइपोलर` (*baipolar*) or `द्विध्रुवी विकार` (*dwidhruvi vikaar*) — the loanword `बाइपोलर` is often more recognizable in everyday use; the formal term is more bookish or clinician-facing.
  Example: `उहाँलाई बाइपोलर भनिएको छ, कहिले धेरै उकासिने र कहिले धेरै निराश हुने हुन्छ।` (*uhaalai baipolar bhaniyeko chha, kahile dherai ukaasine ra kahile dherai niraash hune hunchha.*) — "They have been told they are bipolar; at times they become very elevated and at times very depressed."

- Mania / manic state: `उन्माद` (*unmaad*) or `उन्मादी अवस्था` (*unmaadi awasthaa*) — a formal or literary term for a highly activated, sleepless, overdriven state. It is worth knowing, but many families will not use it spontaneously.
  Example: `यो उन्मादी अवस्था जस्तो देखिए पनि यसको चिकित्सकीय कारण बुझ्नुपर्छ।` (*yo unmaadi awasthaa jasto dekhiye pani yesko chikitsakiya kaaran bujhnuparchha.*) — "Even if this looks like a manic state, we need to understand its medical cause."

- Mood going very high then very low: `मूड धेरै तलमाथि हुने` (*mood dherai talamaathi hune*) or `कहिले धेरै जोशमा, कहिले धेरै निराश हुने` (*kahile dherai joshmaa, kahile dherai niraash hune*) — mixed Nepali-English wording, but often clearer than a technical label.
  Example: `उहाँको मूड धेरै तलमाथि हुन्छ, कहिले धेरै जोशमा र कहिले धेरै खिन्न।` (*uhaako mood dherai talamaathi hunchha, kahile dherai joshmaa ra kahile dherai khinna.*) — "Their mood goes very high and low, sometimes very energized and sometimes very low."

- Overexcited / unusually energized: `जोश चढेको` (*josh chadheko*) or `एकदम जोशमा भएको` (*ekdam joshmaa bhayeko*) — common everyday phrasing for an overly activated or "charged up" state; not specific to bipolar disorder, but often part of how mania gets described.
  Example: `उहाँलाई एकदम जोश चढेको जस्तो छ, बस्नै मान्नुभएको छैन।` (*uhaalai ekdam josh chadheko jasto chha, basnai maannubhayeko chhaina.*) — "It seems like they are extremely keyed up; they will not sit still."

- Sleeping very little without feeling tired: `थोरै सुत्दा पनि पुग्ने` (*thorai sutdaa pani pugne*) or `निद्रा नै नचाहिने जस्तो हुने` (*nindra nai nachaahine jasto hune*) — a very high-yield family description of mania-like activation.
  Example: `उहाँ दुई घण्टा मात्र सुत्नुभयो, तर थाक्नुभएको छैन।` (*uhaa dui ghantaa matra sutnubhayo, tara thaaknubhayeko chhaina.*) — "They slept only two hours, but they are not tired."

- Talking too much / too fast: `धेरै बोलिरहने` (*dherai bolirahane*) or `छिटोछिटो बोल्ने` (*chhito-chhito bolne*) — often one of the first behavioral changes families report.
  Example: `उहाँ धेरै बोलिरहनुहुन्छ र छिटोछिटो कुरा बदलिरहनुहुन्छ।` (*uhaa dherai bolirahanuhunchha ra chhito-chhito kura badalirahanuhunchha.*) — "They keep talking a lot and rapidly changing topics."

- Acting impulsively / without thinking: `सोचविचार नगरी काम गर्ने` (*sochbichaar nagari kaam garne*), `जे मन लाग्यो त्यही गर्ने` (*je man laagyo tyahi garne*), or `जथाभावी खर्च गर्ने` (*jathaabhawi kharcha garne*) — useful for describing manic behavior when a diagnostic label has not been used.
  Example: `उहाँ सोचविचार नगरी खर्च गरिरहनुभएको छ र जे मन लाग्यो त्यही गरिरहनुभएको छ।` (*uhaa sochbichaar nagari kharcha garirahanu bhayeko chha ra je man laagyo tyahi garirahanu bhayeko chha.*) — "They are spending without thinking and doing whatever comes to mind."

- Feeling unusually powerful / able to do anything: `आफूले जे पनि गर्न सक्छु जस्तो लाग्ने` (*aafule je pani garna sakchhu jasto laagne*) — a plain-language way to capture expansive or grandiose thinking.
  Example: `उहाँलाई आफूले जे पनि गर्न सक्छु जस्तो लागिरहेको छ।` (*uhaalai aafule je pani garna sakchhu jasto laagirahiko chha.*) — "They feel like they can do absolutely anything."

- Irritable activated state: `रिस चढ्ने` (*ris chadhne*) or `छिट्टै झर्किने` (*chhittai jharkine*) — some manic states present more as irritability, impatience, or sharpness than happiness.
  Example: `पहिलेभन्दा उहाँलाई छिट्टै रिस चढ्छ र छिट्टै झर्किनुहुन्छ।` (*pahilebhanda uhaalai chhittai ris chadhchha ra chhittai jharkinuhunchha.*) — "Compared with before, they get irritated and angry very quickly."

## 4. Expanded Symptom Language and Meaning Notes

These phrases are especially useful because they appear in common Nepali screening language or map closely to how patients often describe distress in everyday speech.

**Low Mood, Interest, and Hopelessness**

- Sad / low: `दु:खी` (*dukhi*) or `उदास` (*udaas*) — everyday words for feeling sad, low, or emotionally down. `उदास` can also carry the sense of being gloomy or dispirited.
  Example: `म आफू दु:खी र उदास भएको महसुस गरिरहेको छु।` (*ma aafu dukhi ra udaas bhayeko mahasus garirahiko chhu.*) — "I feel sad and low."

- Sadness with detachment / loss of taste for things: `उदासी वा विरक्ति लाग्नु` (*udaasi wa birakti laagnu*) — `उदासी` means sadness, while `विरक्ति` often suggests emotional withdrawal, disinterest, or feeling inwardly turned away from things.
  Example: `मलाई उदासी र विरक्ति लागेको छ।` (*malaai udaasi ra birakti laageko chha.*) — "I feel sad and emotionally withdrawn."

- Inability to feel positive emotion: `कुनै पनि सकारात्मक भावना महसुस गर्न नसक्नु` (*kunai pani sakaaraatmak bhaawanaa mahasus garna nasaknu*) — useful for anhedonia-like states where the person says they cannot feel good feelings at all.
  Example: `मैले कुनै पनि सकारात्मक भावना महसुस गर्न सकेको छैन।` (*maile kunai pani sakaaraatmak bhaawanaa mahasus garna sakeko chhaina.*) — "I have not been able to feel any positive emotions."

- Unable to feel enthusiastic: `उत्साहित बन्न नसक्नु` (*utsaahit banna nasaknu*) — not being able to feel emotionally engaged, eager, or motivated.
  Example: `म कुनै पनि कुरामा उत्साहित बन्न सकिरहेको छैन।` (*ma kunai pani kuraamaa utsaahit banna sakirahiko chhaina.*) — "I am not able to feel enthusiastic about anything."

- Not seeing a reason to feel hopeful: `आशावादी हुनुपर्ने केही कारण नदेख्नु` (*aashaawaadi hunuparne kehi kaaran nadekhnu*) — a useful phrase for low hope without necessarily using a psychiatric label.
  Example: `मलाई आशावादी हुनुपर्ने कुनै कारण देखिँदैन।` (*malaai aashaawaadi hunuparne kunai kaaran dekhiindaina.*) — "I do not see any reason to feel hopeful."

- Life feels meaningless: `जीवन अर्थहीन भएको जस्तो लाग्नु` (*jiwan arthahin bhayeko jasto laagnu*; lit. "to feel as if life has become meaningless") — stronger existential hopelessness.
  Example: `मलाई जीवन अर्थहीन भएको जस्तो लाग्छ।` (*malaai jiwan arthahin bhayeko jasto laagchha.*) — "I feel like life has become meaningless."

- Feeling worthless / useless: `नालायक व्यक्ति जस्तो लाग्नु` (*nalaayak byakti jasto laagnu*) — a strong self-worth term that can signal shame, depression, or self-condemnation.
  Example: `म आफैलाई नालायक व्यक्ति जस्तो महसुस गर्छु।` (*ma aafailai nalaayak byakti jasto mahasus garchhu.*) — "I feel like a worthless person."

- Excessive self-blame: `आफूलाई अनावश्यक रूपमा दोषी ठहराउनु` (*aafulai anawashyak rupmaa doshi thaharaunu*) — blaming oneself unfairly or more than the situation warrants.
  Example: `काम बिग्रँदा म आफूलाई अनावश्यक रूपमा दोषी ठहराउँछु।` (*kaam bigrinda ma aafulai anawashyak rupmaa doshi thaharaaunchhu.*) — "When things go wrong, I blame myself unnecessarily."

**Anxiety, Panic, and Agitation**

- Difficulty relaxing: `तनावमुक्त हुन गाह्रो हुनु` (*tanaabmukta huna gahro hunu*; lit. "hard to become free from tension") — useful when the person cannot settle, decompress, or calm down.
  Example: `मलाई तनावमुक्त हुन निकै गाह्रो भइरहेको छ।` (*malaai tanaabmukta huna nikai gahro bhairahiko chha.*) — "It is very hard for me to relax."

- Inner unrest / agitation: `अशान्त महसुस हुनु` (*ashaanta mahasus hunu*) — feeling inwardly unsettled, disturbed, or unable to settle.
  Example: `म आफैलाई धेरै अशान्त महसुस गर्छु।` (*ma aafailai dherai ashaanta mahasus garchhu.*) — "I feel very unsettled."

- Fear or panic for no clear reason: `बिना कारण डराउने वा घबराउने` (*bina kaaran daraaune wa ghabaraaune*) — fear or panic that feels untriggered or out of proportion to the situation.
  Example: `म बिना कारण डराउने र घबराउने गर्छु।` (*ma bina kaaran daraaune ra ghabaraaune garchhu.*) — "I become frightened and panicky for no clear reason."

- Feeling panicked / terrified: `आतंकित भएको महसुस गर्नु` (*aatankit bhayeko mahasus garnu*) — stronger than ordinary worry; closer to feeling alarmed, panicked, or terrified.
  Example: `मलाई कहिलेकाहीँ आतंकित भएको महसुस हुन्छ।` (*malaai kahilekaahi aatankit bhayeko mahasus hunchha.*) — "Sometimes I feel panicked or terrified."

- Unable to hold oneself together: `आफूलाई समाल्न नसक्नु` (*aafulai samaalna nasaknu*; lit. "unable to manage/hold oneself") — often means not coping well emotionally, not staying composed, or not functioning smoothly.
  Example: `मलाई आफूलाई समाल्न गाह्रो भइरहेको छ।` (*malaai aafulai samaalna gahro bhairahiko chha.*) — "I am having trouble holding myself together."

**Cognitive, Behavioral, and Somatic Symptoms**

- Difficulty starting tasks: `कुनै पनि नयाँ काम आफै सुरु गर्न गाह्रो हुनु` (*kunai pani nayaa kaam aafai suru garna gahro hunu*) — a good phrase for reduced drive, initiation problems, or depressive slowing.
  Example: `मलाई कुनै पनि नयाँ काम आफै सुरु गर्न गाह्रो हुन्छ।` (*malaai kunai pani nayaa kaam aafai suru garna gahro hunchha.*) — "It is hard for me to start even simple tasks on my own."

- Poor concentration: `ध्यान केन्द्रित गर्न गाह्रो हुनु` (*dhyaan kendrit garna gahro hunu*) or `एकाग्र हुन नसक्नु` (*ekaagra huna nasaknu*) — trouble focusing, staying mentally on task, or keeping attention fixed.
  Example: `मलाई ध्यान केन्द्रित गर्न गाह्रो भइरहेको छ।` (*malaai dhyaan kendrit garna gahro bhairahiko chha.*) — "I am having trouble concentrating."

- Overreacting / being too reactive: `चाहिनेभन्दा बढी प्रतिक्रिया दिनु` (*chaahinebhanda badhi pratikriyaa dinu*) — becoming more upset, sharp, or reactive than one intends.
  Example: `सानो कुरामा पनि म चाहिनेभन्दा बढी प्रतिक्रिया दिन्छु।` (*saano kuraamaa pani ma chaahinebhanda badhi pratikriyaa dinchhu.*) — "Even small things make me react more strongly than I should."

- Difficulty sleeping because of distress: `निद्रा नलाग्नु` (*nindra nalaagnu*) — insomnia or inability to fall asleep; in mood contexts this can be tied to sadness, worry, or agitation.
  Example: `म यति दु:खी छु कि मलाई राति निद्रा लाग्दैन।` (*ma yati dukhi chhu ki malaai raati nindra laagdaina.*) — "I am so sad that I cannot sleep at night."

- Tired / low energy / no drive: `थकित महसुस हुनु` (*thakit mahasus hunu*), `ऊर्जा कम हुनु` (*urjaa kam hunu*), or `जाँगर नहुनु` (*jaangar nahunu*; lit. "not having drive/energy") — useful for depressive fatigue.
  Example: `मलाई सधैं थकित महसुस हुन्छ र जाँगर पनि छैन।` (*malaai sadhai thakit mahasus hunchha ra jaangar pani chhaina.*) — "I always feel tired and have no drive."

- Appetite change: `भोक नलाग्नु` (*bhok nalaagnu*) or `पहिलाभन्दा बढी भोक लाग्नु` (*pahilabhandaa badhi bhok laagnu*) — reduced appetite or increased appetite.
  Example: `मलाई भोक नै लाग्दैन।` (*malaai bhok nai laagdaina.*) — "I do not feel hungry at all."

- Feeling emotionally tearful / labile: `भावुक भएको जस्तो महसुस हुनु` (*bhaawuk bhayeko jasto mahasus hunu*) — feeling easily moved, close to tears, or emotionally labile; `भावुक` is not always pathological, so context matters.
  Example: `सानो कुरामा पनि म धेरै भावुक भएको महसुस गर्छु।` (*saano kuraamaa pani ma dherai bhaawuk bhayeko mahasus garchhu.*) — "Even small things make me feel very emotional."

- Crying a lot: `रोइरहनु` (*roirahanu*) — persistent or repeated crying; especially useful in postpartum and depressive screening.
  Example: `म यति दु:खी छु कि म रोइरहन्छु।` (*ma yati dukhi chhu ki ma roirahanchhu.*) — "I am so sad that I keep crying."

**Self-Harm and Suicide**

- Thoughts of harming oneself: `आफूलाई हानि गर्ने विचार` (*aafulai haani garne bichaar*) — broader than suicide; this can include thoughts of self-injury even when the person does not describe an intent to die.
  Example: `आफूलाई हानि गर्ने विचार मेरो मनमा आउँछ।` (*aafulai haani garne bichaar mero manmaa aauchha.*) — "Thoughts of harming myself come into my mind."

- Suicidal thoughts: `आत्महत्या गर्ने विचार` (*aatmahatyaa garne bichaar*) — explicit thoughts about ending one's life. This is more specific and higher-risk than general self-harm language.
  Example: `मलाई आत्महत्या गर्ने विचार आएको छ।` (*malaai aatmahatyaa garne bichaar aayeko chha.*) — "I have had thoughts of suicide."

## 5. Addictions & Substance Abuse

- Addiction (general condition): `लत बसेको` (*lat baseko*; lit. "the habit has settled in") or `दुर्व्यसन` (*durbyasan*) — `लत बसेको` is the more everyday description; `दुर्व्यसन` is more formal and explicitly means harmful addiction or vice.
  Example: `उहाँलाई रक्सीको लत बसेको छ।` (*uhaalai raksiko lat baseko chha.*) — "They have developed an alcohol addiction."

- Bad habit (often used euphemistically for addiction): `नराम्रो बानी` (*naraamro baani*) — often softer or less stigmatizing than directly saying addiction, though it can also minimize severity.
  Example: `परिवारले यसलाई नराम्रो बानी भन्छन्, तर समस्या गम्भीर छ।` (*pariwaarle yeslai naraamro baani bhanchhan, tara samasya gambhir chha.*) — "The family calls it a bad habit, but the problem is serious."

- Addiction rehab center: `सुधार केन्द्र` (*sudhaar kendra*; lit. "improvement center") — a common Nepali label for rehabilitation centers.
  Example: `उहाँ केही समय सुधार केन्द्रमा बस्नुभयो।` (*uhaa kehi samaya sudhaar kendramaa basnubhayo.*) — "They stayed in a rehab center for some time."

**Alcohol & Drinking:**

- Alcohol (liquor/spirits): `रक्सी` (*raksi*) — the default and most widely used term for alcohol.
  Example: `उहाँ रक्सी धेरै खानुहुन्छ।` (*uhaa raksi dherai khaanuhunchha.*) — "They drink a lot of alcohol."

- Local alcohol: `जाँड` (*jaand*), `छ्याङ्` (*chhyaang*), or `तोङ्बा` (*tongba*) — culturally specific alcohol terms; exact use can vary by region and beverage type.
  Example: `उहाँ जाँड र तोङ्बा दुवै पिउनुहुन्छ।` (*uhaa jaand ra tongba duwai piunuhunchha.*) — "They drink both jaand and tongba."

- Alcoholic / heavy drinker: `ज्याँदाहा` (*jyaandahaa*), `रक्सी बाज` (*raksi baaj*), or `रक्सिया` (*raksiyaa*) — all can be socially loaded or stigmatizing; these are important to understand even if a clinician would not usually use them first.
  Example: `गाउँमा कतिपयले उहाँलाई रक्सिया भन्छन्।` (*gaaumaa katipayle uhaalai raksiyaa bhanchhan.*) — "Some people in the village call him an alcoholic."

- Drunk / intoxicated: `मात्तिएको` (*maattiyeko*) or `रक्सी लागेको` (*raksi laageko*; lit. "alcohol has taken hold") — the first is the more direct intoxication word; the second is a common everyday description.
  Example: `उहाँ अहिले मात्तिएको जस्तो देखिनुहुन्छ।` (*uhaa ahile maattiyeko jasto dekhinuhunchha.*) — "They look intoxicated right now."

- Stumbling / unsteady: `हल्लिने` (*halline*) — literally "swaying" or "wobbling"; useful for intoxication as well as weakness.
  Example: `हिँड्दा उहाँ हल्लिनुहुन्थ्यो।` (*hiddaa uhaa hallinuhunthyo.*) — "They were unsteady while walking."

- Drinking openly / heavily: `खोलेर खाने` (*kholera khaane*) — literally "to open and consume"; in context it often implies drinking freely, openly, or in a sustained way.
  Example: `साथीहरूसँग बसेर उहाँ खोलेर खानुहुन्छ।` (*saathiharusanga basera uhaa kholera khaanuhunchha.*) — "When sitting with friends, they drink heavily/openly."

**Drugs & Smoking:**

- Smoking: `चुरोट खाने` (*churot khaane*; lit. "eating cigarettes") — this is the normal Nepali verb phrase for smoking.
  Example: `उहाँले दिनमा धेरै चुरोट खानुहुन्छ।` (*uhaale dinmaa dherai churot khaanuhunchha.*) — "They smoke many cigarettes a day."

- Hard drugs / illicit drugs: `लागू पदार्थ` (*laagu padaartha*) or `ड्रग` (*drag*) — `लागू पदार्थ` is the formal umbrella term; `ड्रग` is the common English loanword.
  Example: `उहाँ लागू पदार्थ प्रयोग गर्नुहुन्थ्यो।` (*uhaa laagu padaartha prayog garnuhunthyo.*) — "They used illicit drugs."

- Marijuana / cannabis: `गाँजा` (*gaanjaa*) — younger speakers may also use `विड` (*wid*, from "weed") or `जोइन्ट` (*joint*).
  Example: `केही युवाहरू यसलाई गाँजा भन्दा विड भन्छन्।` (*kehi yuwaaharu yeslai gaanjaa bhanda wid bhanchhan.*) — "Some young people call it weed rather than gaanja."

- Hashish: `चरस` (*charas*) — a widely recognized term distinct from plain `गाँजा` in common speech.
  Example: `उहाँ चरस पनि प्रयोग गर्नुहुन्थ्यो भन्ने परिवारले बतायो।` (*uhaa charas pani prayog garnuhunthyo bhanne pariwaarle bataayo.*) — "The family reported that they also used hashish."

**Withdrawal & Cessation (Quitting):**

- To quit / stop using: `छोड्ने` (*chhodne*) or `त्याग्ने` (*tyaagne*) — `छोड्ने` is everyday speech; `त्याग्ने` is more formal and can sound more deliberate or moral in tone.
  Example: `उहाँले चुरोट छोड्ने प्रयास गरिरहनुभएको छ।` (*uhaale churot chhodne prayaas garirahnubhayo chha.*) — "They are trying to quit smoking."

- To get clean / become free from addiction: `कुलतबाट मुक्त हुने` (*kulatbaat mukta hune*; lit. "to become free from bad habits/addiction") — a strong recovery-oriented phrase.
  Example: `उहाँ कुलतबाट मुक्त हुन चाहनुहुन्छ।` (*uhaa kulatbaat mukta huna chaahanuhunchha.*) — "They want to become free from addiction."

- Withdrawal / craving: `तलतल लाग्ने` (*talatal laagne*) — a very useful colloquial phrase for craving, urge, or the gnawing pull toward a substance; the literal feel is something like an internal itch or pull.
  Example: `चुरोट नखाँदा मलाई तलतल लाग्छ।` (*churot nakhaandaa malaai talatal laagchha.*) — "When I do not smoke, I get strong cravings."
